Getting Started With Running


For Beginners.
Though running seems like such an easy activity (you are only moving faster than walking), it needs to be treated as an actual sport. If one has a sedentary lifestyle, it is best to start off with jogging slowly a little bit and then gradually running more miles in order to prevent potential injuries.

TOP TIP

The more gradually you ease into running, in terms of time, days, and/or miles, the lower chance you have to receive an injury. Some experts recommend a jog-walk method where you alternate between each for 1-8 minutes.

EXAMPLE SCHEDULE

Mon: walk 20 minutes
Tues: jog-walk alternate 5 min 4x
Wed: walk 20 minutes
Thurs: jog 20 minutes
Fri: walk 20 minutes
Sat: jog 20 minutes
Sun: rest

GRADUAL & CONSISTENT

After you get comfortable with an easy run-walk schedule, slowly increase the minutes you run while decreasing the minutes you walk.

JUST IN CASE

If you notice at any time that you have persistent leg pain or foot pain, try decreasing the intensity of your training schedule and walk more than you run. Then, after a while, slowly run more while walking less.
